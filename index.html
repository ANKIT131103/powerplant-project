01Current Situation & Challenge
The Maldives — 1,190 coral islands across 90,000 km² of Indian Ocean — faces a critical energy paradox: it sits under one of Earth's most intense solar irradiance belts (5.5–6.1 kWh/m²/day) yet imports 100% of its energy as diesel fuel, spending over $310M/year. Rising seas make this not just economic — it is a matter of national survival.

Solar potential
5.9 kWh/m²/day
Equatorial position at 4°N guarantees near-constant sun angles with minimal seasonal swing (±0.4 kWh/m²/day). SW Monsoon cloud cover is offset by simultaneous wind boost.
Wind potential
6.2 m/s avg
Consistent Indian Ocean trade winds. Peak during SW Monsoon (May–Sep) — exactly when solar dips. This natural anti-correlation is a key design advantage.
OTEC potential
22°C ΔT
Ocean surface at 27°C vs deep water at ~5°C. OTEC produces 24/7 baseload electricity with zero fuel. Viable at commercial scale by 2030 for Malé deep-water sites.

06Energy Storage Architecture
Storage is the most critical enabler. With 200+ isolated island grids, the design stacks three layers: fast Li-Ion BESS for frequency response (milliseconds), medium-duration flow batteries for 8–12h overnight, and green hydrogen for multi-day / seasonal backup. Each island must be self-sufficient for 16+ hours without mainland connection.

Phase 1 — Foundation
Malé Metro Transformation

  • 80 MW rooftop + floating solar (Malé, Hulhumalé, Thilafushi)
  • 480 MWh Li-Ion BESS (2 grid-scale sites)
  • SCADA smart grid upgrade — full Malé cluster
  • Retire 40% diesel; keep 30 MW spinning reserve
  • Establish MNEA regulatory authority
  • 800 local technicians trained (solar, BESS, operations)
  • LNG bridge fuel contract for gap coverage
⚡ 55% renewable — Malé Metro by 2027
Phase 2 — National Roll-Out
Atolls + Offshore Wind
2027–2030 · $720M CAPEX
  • 120 MW offshore wind (Addu, Lhaviyani, Baa lagoon zones)
  • 196 outer island solar + BESS kits deployed
  • 500km HVDC submarine cable — Malé to Addu
  • Floating solar: 8 resort lagoons (50 MW)
  • Green hydrogen pilot: 5 MW electrolyser, Hulhumalé
  • All government ferries + vehicles → EV; V2G integration
  • Vanadium redox flow battery deployment (420 MWh)
🌊 82% renewable nationally by 2030

  • Carbon credit programme live — est. $28M/year
  • Malé–Colombo subsea cable pre-feasibility study

13Water–Energy Nexus
The Maldives has no natural freshwater bodies. All drinking water comes from rainwater harvesting and diesel-powered reverse osmosis (RO) desalination. Coupling solar power with RO desalination creates a double win: cheaper water AND cheaper electricity, with desalination plants acting as flexible demand-response loads to absorb midday solar surplus.
